Anotaciones de Cascades—Greg Satell

It takes a network to defeat a network.

Ironically, the strategies of General McChrystar in Iraq and the Otpor activists in Servia were surprisingly similar. Both focused on weaving networks that were resilient, adaptable an based on shared values Once that was archieved, the specific tactics employed, to a large extent, took care of themselves.

El poder se ha movido de las jerarquías a las redes. Ese poder sólo puede usarse efectivamente con planeación y fortalecimiento de las redes.

Para que la burocracia sobreviva, debe convertirse en red.

It is not “influential” people who create change but […] “easily influenced people influencing other easily influenced people” that creates a cascade.

Para invitar al cambio, se necesita un pretexto consciente.

Revolutions don’t begin with a slogan, they begin with a cause.

  • ¿Obrador haciendo del combate a la corrupción una causa?

Encontrar esta causa unificadora suele ser mucho trabajo. Después debe haber un plan. El plan debe intentar quitar soporte a la contraparte, ganar aliados.

Small groups, as it turns out, are almos infinitely scalable.

Lo primero que hay que hacer para promover un cambio es crear un ecosistema de conexiones.

In many cases, it only takes a few rock throwers among a cast of thousands to discredit an entire movement. Anarchy isn’t a value, but simply a lack of discipline.

Los valores permiten tomar decisiones consistentes e informadas. (esta idea no está exactamente en el libro.) Los grupos coordinados necesitan valores para actuar en conjunto.

Once “I have a Dream” devolved into “Any Means Necessary”, progress stalled, because values are not salver for the far left of the Spectrum of Allies, designed to rile up the most active supporters, but are vehicles of shared purpose and shared consciousness that allow you to connect even to those who are not committed to your struggle and may even be passively opposed.

Paying the costs associated with values by appealing to deeper truths creates the shared purpose and shared consciousness that leads to empowerment. It also signals to those outside your movement that you’re serious, not just an undisciplined band of rabble rousers

The way you communicate those values is through platforms and tactics.

The problem is not that the world is too corrupt or that the status quo is too ingrained

Shaunin continually stressed to me how important it is to do the groundwork early, long before a window of opportunity opens up, to drive the reforms through and make them a reality. It is that early work that helps you build up expertise so that you are able to propose detailed, viable reforms and win the credibility to gain support from both internal stakeholders and international institutions.

Make no mistake, successful movements do not overpower, they attract.

Unsuccessful movements rally the faithful and demonize those who don’t share their ideas or their commitment. They make their point but fail to make a difference.

Remember that disruptive innovations are esentially solutions looking for problems. That’s why they are so different from other kinds of innovations. When we pursue dirsuptive innovations, we are generally not trying to discover new technologies or improve on existing products, but rather to find a market for a technology that already exists. That’s why disruptive innovations almost always require a new business model.

La innovación disruptiva no es la panacea.

After 20 years of innovation and product development [at Google], its core business still makes up for 99 percent of the company’s revenue.

Many people think of innovation as discrding the old to make room for the new, but Google shows that even a highly successfull, forward-looking technology company earns most of its money from the older products in its core businesses. Its “Other Bets,” while growing fast, amount to just a drop in the bucket.

As we saw in the case of Xerox, just because you are coming out with “new and improved” products every year doesn’t mean your business is safe.
